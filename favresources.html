Podcasts

One of the great things about language learning is that there are so many engaging ways to develop your skills. If you want to practice your Spanish comprehension skills, podcasts are a great way to learn and immerse yourself without using much mental energy. Podcasts are wonderful because you can listen to them while doing other simple tasks that aren’t too mentally demanding; cleaning, driving, gaming, exercising, and so on. This makes Spanish podcasts a great learning tool for all learners, but especially for those who don’t want to devote a lot of time and effort into language learning.

Duolingo Spanish Podcast

There are two things that make this podcast unique. Firstly, while this is a language learning podcast, there are no lessons and there isn’t any teaching of language concepts. Duolingo uses stories to help the listener practice their Spanish comprehension. The second thing that makes this podcast unique is that it’s a bilingual podcast. In these episodes, we hear the storyteller speaking to us with a clearer and slightly slower Spanish than the casual Spanish that native speakers might typically use. But the episodes are designed to be learning tools, so we also receive lots of context in English. This context is very effective because it summarizes the main ideas, explains foreign concepts, and teaches vocab that listeners are likely not familiar with.

Background

For those of you who really want to know more about me, this section is for you. A big part of my identity is my Polish family background. Both of my parents lived in Poland before leaving everything behind for a life in the United States. As a child of two Polish parents, I struggled to learn English when I started attending school in the US. Unfortunately, I didn’t even manage to attain a competent level of biliteracy after going through that struggle as I am not able to carry any real or meaningful conversations in Polish.

I would say that my English has been at a native level since late elementary school, and while I understand a lot of Polish, I find it difficult to express my ideas through it. Interestingly, I have tried to learn more Polish but I’m not consistent with it. In fact, I spend infinitely more time trying to learn Spanish than Polish. I probably put so much time into Spanish is because, unlike Polish, there are many resources that are available and it’s easy to learn if you’re an English speaker. Either way I should probably pick up the slack, my Polish ancestors are probably shaking their heads right now.

As you can probably tell from my last picture, I usually don’t like to take myself too seriously. I’m relatively carefree and relaxed, and I enjoy being around people. When I’m alone, I like to spend time doing things that interest me, and there are a lot of things that interest me. Of course, one of those things is language learning, but I also enjoy working on my car, playing games like Skyrim, working on this website, going to the gym, and making/playing music.
